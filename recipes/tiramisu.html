Tiramisu

Description

A classic Italian dessert with layers of espresso-soaked ladyfingers and a silky mascarpone cream, finished with bitter cocoa. It’s rich yet airy, no‑bake, and tastes even better after resting in the fridge.

Ingredients

  • Ladyfingers: 250 g (about 24–30 biscuits)
  • Espresso or strong coffee: 300 ml, cooled (add 2–3 tbsp Marsala or dark rum, optional)
  • Mascarpone: 500 g
  • Egg yolks: 4 large
  • Granulated sugar: 100 g
  • Egg whites: 3 large (or 120 ml heavy cream, whipped, if avoiding raw whites)
  • Vanilla extract: 1 tsp
  • Cocoa powder (unsweetened): 2–3 tbsp, for dusting
  • Pinch of salt
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional)

Steps

  1. Brew and cool the coffee: Pull espresso or make strong coffee. Stir in Marsala/rum (optional) and let it cool completely.
  2. Yolks + sugar: In a bowl, whisk the egg yolks with sugar until pale and thick, 2–3 minutes.
  3. Mascarpone cream: Fold mascarpone and vanilla into the yolk mixture until smooth.
  4. Aerate:
    • Option A (classic): Whisk egg whites with a pinch of salt to soft peaks and gently fold into the mascarpone mixture.
    • Option B (no raw whites): Whip cold heavy cream to soft peaks and fold in instead.
  5. Dip ladyfingers: Quickly dip each biscuit in the cooled coffee (1–2 seconds per side; don’t soak). Arrange a snug layer in your dish.
  6. Layer: Spread half the mascarpone cream over the first layer. Repeat with another dipped-ladyfinger layer and the remaining cream.
  7. Chill: Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours (overnight is best) to set and develop flavor.
  8. Finish: Dust generously with cocoa powder before serving; add chocolate shavings if desired.
